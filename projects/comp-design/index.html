Computer Design

A design of a computer with NAND gate chips.


Project in Progress

GITHUB
DOWNLOAD

Concept:

Originally, the goal of this project was to design a computer using complementary PMOS and NMOS transistors, however the cost of purchasing the discrete transistors proved prohibitive, so NAND gates were selected as a cheaper alternative to implement the logic.

Design phase:

The first step of the design process is the determination of the processor instruction set and operation bit length. A good balance between performance and price was a variable length of 8 bits and an op code of 4 bits:

Instruction Operation
ADD Y A B Adds A and B and puts the result in register Y.
ADDC Y A B Adds A and B with the carry in from the carry register and puts the result in register Y.
SUB Y A B Subtracts B from A and puts the result in register Y.
SUBC Y A B Subtracts B from A with the carry in from the carry register and puts the result in register Y.
MULT Y A B Multiplies A and B and puts the result in register Y.
DIV Y A B Divides A by B and puts the result in register Y.
NAND Y A B NANDs A and B and puts the result in register Y.
OR Y A B ORs A and B and puts the result in register Y.
NOT Y A NOTs A and puts the result in register Y.
XOR Y A B XORs A and B and puts the result in register Y.
LEA A B Loads the address of A into register B.
JMP FUNC A Jumps to the position given by A. FUNC gives options for conditional jumps.
